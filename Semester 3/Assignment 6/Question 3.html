Traffic Awareness

Traffic rules to be followed

  1. Walk on the right side of the road.
  2. Use pedestrian crossing to cross the road.
  3. Look on both side of the road before you cross.
  4. Don't play on the road.
  5. Stop driving if you feel sleepy or tired.
  6. Don't use mobile phones while driving.
  7. Avoid over speed.
  8. Avoid alcohol while driving.
  9. Always wear helmet/seat belts.
  10. Give priority to pedestrians at zebra crossing.

Traffic Signals

Signal Description Details
Stop A stop traffic signal is a vital road safety device that commands vehicles to halt, preventing collisions and ensuring orderly flow of traffic.
No Entry A "No Entry" sign is a traffic signal indicating that vehicles are prohibited from entering a particular road or area, ensuring safe and controlled traffic flow.
No Stopping A "No Stopping" sign indicates that vehicles are prohibited from halting or parking at any time in the specified area, ensuring a smooth and uninterrupted flow of traffic.
No Stopping or Standing A "No Stopping or Standing" sign indicates that vehicles are prohibited from halting or waiting in the designated area, ensuring that traffic flows smoothly and minimizing potential obstructions.
Right Hand Curve A "Right Hand Curve" sign indicates that the road ahead curves sharply to the right, warning drivers to reduce speed and navigate the turn carefully to ensure safety.
Left Hand Curve A "Left Hand Curve" sign indicates that the road ahead curves sharply to the left, alerting drivers to slow down and navigate the turn carefully for safety.
Over Taking Prohibited "Overtaking Prohibited" means vehicles can't pass each other in this area for safety.
Pedestrian Prohibited A "Pedestrian Prohibited" sign indicates that pedestrians are not allowed to walk in the designated area, ensuring safety and preventing accidents.
Y intersection A "Y Intersection" sign indicates that the road ahead splits into two branches forming a "Y" shape, alerting drivers to the upcoming fork in the road and the need to choose a direction.
Speed Limit A "Speed Limit" sign indicates the maximum speed at which vehicles are allowed to travel in the specified area, promoting road safety and ensuring consistent traffic flow.
