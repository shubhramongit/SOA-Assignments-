Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, often described as a symbol of love, stands majestically on the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra, India. Built between 1632 and 1653 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, this white marble mausoleum is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most celebrated architectural masterpieces in the world. The Taj Mahal's intricate details, including its marble inlay work with precious stones, elegant domes, and lush gardens, reflect the pinnacle of Mughal architecture. The harmonious proportions and the exquisite symmetry of the structure, along with its enchanting reflections in the surrounding pools, create a breathtaking spectacle. The Taj Mahal's timeless beauty continues to attract millions of visitors, making it a quintessential emblem of India's rich cultural heritage and history.
